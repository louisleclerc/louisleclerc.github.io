About me

I've worked for ten years in market research in a wide range of areas: transportation, public works, telecommunication, energy, health, finance, media, government, and NGOs.
I've also collaborated with academic research projects in computer science and digital humanities.

My stack is simple yet efficient. I mostly use Python (SkLearn, SciPy, NetworkX, Pandas, Numpy, Matplotlib, Seaborn, BeautifulSoup, Selenium, Scrapy, Urllib), as well as a bit of SQL, JavaScript, HTML/CSS and Shell scripts. I'm using Linux, Gephi and all the open-source material I can find.

My philosophy is to seek constant improvement. I believe oversimplified explanations are never the answer. The key to a better future lies in a more profound truth. I learn from others, and I also like to think they learn from me. My very own personal motto could be something like:

Absolute certainty is asymptote-shaped:
You'll never reach it, but you get closer every time you try!
Some themes I am intensely interested in are transportation and urban planning, economics, and children's development. I'm also an amateur triathlete, Half Iron Man finisher, bass player, occasional photographer, intermittent gamer, and many more.
